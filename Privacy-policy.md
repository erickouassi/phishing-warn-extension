### **Privacy Policy**

**Last Updated:** July 15, 2026

PhishGuard ("the Extension") is designed to help users detect and avoid potentially phishing websites. We take your privacy seriously.

### 1. Information We Collect
**We do not collect any personal information.**

The extension operates entirely on your device and does not send any data to external servers except for the following optional feature:

- **External Safe List** (optional): The extension can fetch a JSON file from a URL that you specify in the source code. This file contains only a list of domain names you trust. No other data is transmitted.

### 2. Permissions & Data Access
- **URL Analysis**: The extension reads the URL of the websites you visit **locally on your device** to detect suspicious patterns.
- **Warning Banner**: When a suspicious site is detected, the extension injects a simple warning banner on that page.
- **Storage**: The extension uses Chrome’s local storage only to save your personal safe list (whitelist) of trusted domains.
- **No Tracking**: We do not track your browsing history, collect any personal data, or use analytics.

### 3. Third Parties
The only possible external connection is to the **user-defined safe list JSON URL** (which you control). No third-party tracking, advertising, or analytics services are used.

### 4. Data Sharing
We do not share, sell, or disclose any user data because no personal data is collected.

### 5. Your Control
- You can remove the extension at any time via Chrome’s extensions page.
- You can clear your local safe list by removing the extension or clearing Chrome’s extension data.
- You control whether to use the external safe list feature.

### 6. Children’s Privacy
This extension is not directed at children under the age of 13 and does not knowingly collect data from children.

### 7. Changes to this Policy
We may update this Privacy Policy occasionally. The latest version will be available within the extension or on our website (if any).
